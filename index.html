Our proprietary frameworks

Named Systems. Proven Methodology. Measurable Results.

Each framework is a defined, deployable system — built from decades of clinical and educational practice and refined through institutional-level delivery.

Clinical

Clinical Infrastructure Assessment (CIA)

A structured organizational audit of ABA and special education clinical operations — evaluating program design, documentation standards, scheduling integrity, supervisory systems, and compliance posture across the organization.

Clinical

ITP Standardization Framework (ISF)

A center-wide or enterprise-wide framework for consistent, high-quality Individual Treatment Plan development — ensuring clinical standards, goal architecture, and program design are unified across every clinician and location.

Clinical

7.5-Minute Service Delivery System (SDS)

A precision scheduling and service delivery methodology that ensures clinical goals are systematically targeted, tracked, and executed within every session — eliminating inconsistency and maximizing clinical outcomes.

Education

Special Education Systems Readiness Assessment (SESRA)

An institutional evaluation framework for state agencies, large school districts, and government ministries — assessing current special education infrastructure, workforce capacity, compliance alignment, and readiness for systemic reform.

Workforce

National Workforce Development Framework (NWDF)

A comprehensive model for building sustainable special education and ABA workforce pipelines — from foundational educator training and competency frameworks to advanced practitioner development and institutional train-the-trainer programs.

International

Government Readiness & Policy Assessment (GRPA)

A structured engagement framework designed for ministries of education and national government agencies — assessing current special education policy, infrastructure gaps, workforce readiness, and the strategic pathway to national system implementation.

Compliance

Multi-Site Quality Assurance System (MQAS)

An enterprise quality and compliance framework for multi-site ABA providers and health systems — standardizing documentation, supervisory review, clinical auditing, and regulatory alignment across all locations and jurisdictions.

Advisory

Institutional Implementation Methodology (IIM)

The overarching engagement methodology governing every Pinnacle EDS engagement — defining phased delivery, governance structure, stakeholder alignment, risk management, and long-term sustainability planning for institutional-scale engagements.
